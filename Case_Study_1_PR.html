Executive Summary

The following report is an analysis of craft beer and breweries in the United States. The analysis was based on a sample data of 2410 beers and 558 brewries. The analysis below covers the following:

  • How many breweries there are per state.
  • An analysis that identifies missing values from the data sets provided.
  • The computed median alcohol content and median bitterness unit for each state.
  • Identification of the beer with the highest alchol by volume (ABV) and the most bitter beer (IBU).
  • Summary statistics of alchol by volume (ABV).
  • Analysis of alchol content vs bitterness to identify any relationship between the two.

Observations: There are an average of 10.9 craft breweries per-state in the US. Colorado leads with 47 brewries including Upslope Brewing Company in Boulder which produces the beer with the highest alcohol content “Lee Hill Series Vol. 5” a Belgian Style Quadrupel Ale that’s 13% alcohol by volume. The average ABV of craft beers represented in this data is .059 or 6% alchol by volume. Oregon is home to the bitterest beer “Bitter Bitch Imperial IPA” produced by Astoria Brewing Company it’s IBU is 138. There also seems to be a relationship between alcohol content and bitterness. The analysis below shows that in general the higher the alcohol content the higher the IBU or bitterness.
